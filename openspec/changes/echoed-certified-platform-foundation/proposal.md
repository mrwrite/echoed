# EchoEd Certified Platform Foundation

## Why

EchoEd currently provides a strong foundation for curriculum delivery, progress tracking, and role-based learning experiences. However, it does not yet support the structure, validation, and outcome measurement required for a certified educational platform.

To become a trusted platform for African American and African diaspora education, EchoEd must support structured learning paths, measurable outcomes, assessments, and certification. Additionally, curriculum must align with clear learning objectives, and the user experience must reflect cultural identity, accessibility, and trust.

## What Changes

- Introduce program-based learning paths that group courses into structured certification tracks
- Add an assessment and scoring system tied to lessons and programs
- Implement a certification engine based on completion and performance criteria
- Align curriculum with learning objectives, standards, and measurable outcomes
- Improve platform UX to support clarity, trust, accessibility, and cultural identity

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `programs-and-learning-paths`
- `assessment-and-scoring`
- `certification-engine`
- `curriculum-standards-alignment`
- `platform-experience-upgrade`

### Modified Capabilities
- `progress-and-course-completion`
- `lesson-delivery-and-activity-system`
- `app-shell-and-navigation`

## Impact

- Backend: new models, relationships, and extensions to existing progress and lesson systems
- Frontend: new flows for programs, assessments, and certification display
- Curriculum: structured transformation of existing content into outcome-driven programs
- UX: improvements to onboarding, navigation, readability, and trust signals
- Testing: new and updated tests across learning, assessment, and certification flows
